About Me

I’m Nadine Hamzeh, a fifth-year Computer Engineering student obsessed with the seam where hardware precision meets software elegance. Over the last four years I’ve split my time between the lab oscilloscope and the command line, hacking together everything from RTL‐level FPGA designs to cloud-native inference pipelines. I thrive on deep dives—whether that’s wrangling Verilog timing constraints or squeezing the last clock cycle out of a C++ SIMD kernel.

My current focus is edge-AI acceleration: building lightweight CNN accelerators that turn a cheap microcontroller into a real-time vision system. I’ve taped-out a RISC-V SoC in Skywater 130 nm, authored Linux device-tree overlays for custom GPIO IP, and optimised PyTorch models for quantised deployment with TVM. When things break (and they always do) I start with the scope probe, end with perf, and document every micro-watt I shave in between.

  • Digital design: VHDL/Verilog, Quartus & Vivado, AMBA AXI-4 interconnect
  • Embedded systems: STM32, ESP32, bare-metal C & RTOS, Zephyr
  • High-performance C++: Eigen, OpenMP, vector intrinsics, cache-aware data layouts
  • AI toolchain: PyTorch ➜ ONNX ➜ TVM, mixed-precision quantisation, INT8 GEMM kernels
  • Dev-ops: Docker-Compose, GitHub Actions, IaC with Terraform
  • Research flair: co-authored a conference poster on energy-aware CNN pruning; reviewer for IEEE Embedded Systems Letters

Short version? Give me a tough problem and a deadline—I’ll hand you a working prototype and a performance report that makes the baseline look silly.
